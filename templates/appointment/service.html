Our Services


Dentistry

The profession concerned with the prevention and treatment of oral disease, including diseases of the teeth and supporting structures and diseases of the soft tissues of the mouth.

Cardiology

A branch of medicine that specializes in diagnosing and treating diseases of the heart, blood vessels, and circulatory system. These diseases include coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems, and heart failure.

ENT Specialists

Special training in diagnosing and treating diseases of the ear, nose, and throat. Also called otolaryngologist.

Neuroanatomy

The anatomy of nervous tissue and the nervous system.the study of the structures and relationships among the various parts of the nervous system.

Blood Screening

To detect markers of infection in order to prevent the release of infected blood and blood components for clinical use. Blood screening strategies are designed to assure the safety of blood units, but should not be used for notifying blood donors of reactive test results.

Eye Care

Early treatment is critically important to prevent some common eye diseases from causing permanent vision loss or blindness

Physical Therapy

Therapy for the preservation, enhancement, or restoration of movement and physical function impaired or threatened by disease, injury, or disability that utilizes therapeutic exercise, physical modalities (such as massage and electrotherapy), assistive devices, and patient education and training.
